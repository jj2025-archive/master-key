### 10 Ideas (made by prompting Claude to treat my GitHub repositories as "vibrant ideas" and generate new concepts by combining them):

### 1. **Tired Eyes App**
When you travel across time zones, your eyes get tired and colors look weird. This app learns your travel pattern and tells you which colors are easiest to read at different times of day. Like: "It's 2am in Singapore, use yellow text on dark backgrounds."

### 2. **Fake News Energy Filter**
Your brain gets tired from reading stressful news. This app learns when you're mentally exhausted and automatically hides heavy/upsetting stories. Instead shows lighter stuff when you're drained, hard-hitting stories when you're sharp.

### 3. **Cultural Display Tuner**
Same video looks different to someone in Tokyo vs. New York because of lighting and display tech. This adjusts your screen automatically so a video from that archive looks right *to you*, wherever you are.

### 4. **AI Video Lie Detector**
AI can fake videos now (Sora). This tool watches AI-generated videos and spots when they look "off"—flickering, color weirdness, things that don't quite match. Helps you know if a video is real or computer-made.

### 5. **Work Mood Helper for Remote Teams**
When you're working from different countries, your brain gets foggy from too many Zoom calls and Slack messages. This dashboard says: "You're scattered right now—do async work instead of meetings" or "Switch to simpler tasks."

### 6. **Smart Screen That Adapts to Your Brain**
Your monitor notices when you're getting mentally tired and automatically makes colors less bright and screen refresh smoother (less flickery). Like turning down a spotlight when your eyes hurt.

### 7. **Social Media Time Capsule Checker**
Someone archives their old Facebook posts from when they lived in different countries. This tool finds which posts are real memories vs. exaggerated/fake moments. Shows timeline of where misinformation crept in.

### 8. **AI Video Reality Check**
Compare a real news video to what an AI *could* fake. If they look too similar, it's a red flag—means the real footage might be easy to deepfake later.

### 9. **AI That Understands Pictures Like Words**
Right now, AI reads text great but struggles with images. This teaches AI to think of images like sentences—combining colors, movement, and meaning together. So it can spot manipulated photos better.

### 10. **Your Perfect Work Setup Finder**
The app learns: "You work best at 10am in coffee shops with cool lighting and lo-fi music." It tracks which countries/times/colors make you most creative, then recommends when to travel or work based on *when you're at your best*.
